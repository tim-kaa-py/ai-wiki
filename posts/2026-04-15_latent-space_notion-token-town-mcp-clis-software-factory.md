🤖 3 takeaways I picked from "Notion's Token Town: 5 Rebuilds, 100+ Tools, MCP vs CLIs and the Software Factory Future" (Latent Space) — summary generated by Claude Code:

▸ Coding agents are the kernel of AGI. The thesis: "everything becomes a coding agent." Design so your agents can bootstrap, debug, and maintain their own software. When adding a new capability, prefer giving the agent a sandbox + filesystem + code-writing ability over a bespoke deterministic tool.

▸ Turn your eval framework into an agent harness. An agent downloads the dataset, runs the eval, inspects failures, proposes fixes, implements them — humans observe the outer loop. Wire your eval framework to be CLI-drivable; let a coding agent write your next eval the way it writes your next unit test.

▸ Rebuild your harness on a cadence. Simon Last: "I'm basically just rewriting everything in a loop every six months." Low-ego teams that delete their own code move faster on frontier. Budget for periodic framework rewrites; structure forms after ships, not before.
